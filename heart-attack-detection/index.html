The Silent Threat

What is a Silent Heart Attack?

Unlike classic heart attacks with severe chest pain, a silent myocardial infarction (SMI) lacks typical symptoms. Patients might only feel mild fatigue or indigestion, delaying life-saving treatment.

Our Solution

By continuously monitoring ECG patterns, blood oxygen (SpO2), body temperature, and patient motion, our AI algorithm identifies anomalies that predict an SMI before irreversible damage occurs.

Risk Management & Reconditioning

Tachycardia

Criterion: Resting HR > 100 bpm

Recommended Protocol:

  • Guided Pranayama: 10 mins of slow, deep diaphragmatic breathing to enhance parasympathetic tone.
  • Light Aerobics: 15-20 mins of flat-surface walking at a mild pace, keeping heart rate within zone 1.
  • Vagal Maneuvers: Gentle progressive relaxation starting from facial muscles to the core.

Mild Hypoxia

Criterion: SpO2 levels 90% - 94%

Recommended Protocol:

  • Pursed-Lip Breathing: Inhale deeply for 2 seconds, exhale for 4 seconds through pursed lips.
  • Incentive Spirometry: 10 guided breaths per hour to expand pulmonary capacity effectively.
  • Posture Adjustment: Sit upright in an orthopneic position to maximize chest cavity expansion.

Hypertension Risk

Criterion: Sustained BP > 140/90 mmHg

Recommended Protocol:

  • Isometric Exercises: Gentle handgrip exercises at 30% max effort for 2 mins to lower resting BP.
  • Mindfulness Meditation: Daily 15 mins focus sessions to significantly reduce sympathetic nerve drive.
  • Endurance Training: Stationary cycling or aquatic workouts after clearance from medical personnel.
